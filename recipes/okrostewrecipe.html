Okro Stew Recipe


The famous Ghanaian okro stew

Devoting our attention every now and then to eat is essential for our very existence. The Ghanaian style okro stew is a local favorite and definitely worth this devotion we speak of. It is made with okra vegetables cooked in a delicious amalgamation of palm oil, meat (optional), along with other vegetables and African spices. Okra is a nutirent powerhouse, packed full of vitamins such as folate and vitamin c, as well as soluble and isoluble fibre.

As a pot one pot recipe, it is slimy in nature, which might be a turn off for some However, its slimy nature, as well as high mineral and fiber content is essential for reducing the risk of heart disease, as well as keeping the intestinal tract health. If you are interested in how to prepare this sumptious and healthy meal, you are at the right place.

Follow the steps below to find out how.

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs fresh Okra
  • 1 lb smoked fish
  • 4 lbs meat
  • 2 large onions
  • 4 medium-sized tomatoes
  • 4 habanero peppers,75g ginger, 2 garlic cloves
  • 4 fingers of herrings
  • 1 bunch jute leaves
  • 2 cups of palm oil
  • 1 beef (or chicken) stock cube
  • salt

Steps

  1. Chop up the meat into bite sized chunks and place into a pot. Chop one onion and add it together with 2 habanero peppers to the beef.
  2. Add the beef or chicken stock cube, some water and cook until tender.
  3. While the meat cooks, finely chop up the second onion. Heat up the palm oil in a pot and fry the onions until they begin to caramelize.
  4. Add diced ginger and garlic to fry for about a minute, then add the two chopped tomatoes.
  5. Blend the remaining tomatoes and onion, with ginger and 2 habanero peppers.
  6. Pour the blended mixture over the caramelized onions and allow the sauce to cook for about 10 minutes. Stir regularly to prevent burning.
  7. Break the smoked fish into chunks and add it to the stew with a little water, and allow to simmer for 5 minutes.
  8. Chop the okra into thin slices and add it to the stew and stir. Add more water to cook the okra well for about 10 more minutes.
  9. Add the cooked meat along with the stock and stir. Let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  10. Add salt if required and there you have it! The dish is ready to be served!
