Kontomire Stew Recipe


Kontomire stew is a popular Ghanaian dish that can be enjoyed with rice, plantain, yam, banku, gari, to name a few. The main ingredient is the kontomire, which is basically cocoyam leaves.

Let us go through the steps to make this sumptious and healthy dish.

What you will need

  • Kontomire leaves (about 15)
  • 6 tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup of turkey berries
  • 3 large onions
  • Pepper
  • 2 smoked salmon
  • 1 cup melon seeds
  • 1 cup palm oil
  • 1 salted fish
  • 2 teaspoons grounded shrimp
  • salt as required

Steps

  1. Soak melon seeds in water and set aside for later.
  2. To prevent itching when cutting, wash kontomire leaves with salt water solution. Go ahead and cut the kontomire leaves into desired stripes.
  3. Put the cut leaves into a pot and fill with little water and boil for about 5 minutes. Set aside after boiling.
  4. Blend 2 onions, tomatoes, pepper, and turkey berries together.
  5. Remove the flesh of the salmon, break them into sizeable chunks and set aside.
  6. Add 1/4 spoon of salt and enough water to the soaked melon seeds, and blend into a paste.
  7. Heat up some palm oil in a saucepan, add sliced onions and saleted fish. When the fried onions are softenend, add the blended tomatoes, onions, peppers and turkey berries and let them simmer on medium heat.
  8. Add the salmon, salt and grounded shrimp and let it cook for about 10 minutes.
  9. Add the blended melon seeds next but DO NOT STIR yet. Cover and let it simmer for another 10 minutes.
  10. Finally add the kontomire and gently stir together, adding any remaining sliced onions until they are softened. Your meal is ready at this point and can be served with yam, rice, cocoyam, plantains or banku!
