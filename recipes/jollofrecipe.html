Coconut Jollof Rice Recipe


Jollof rice is a very popular dish in West Africa. The countries of Ghana and Nigeria have for several years, engaged in friendly banter over whose jollof rice is better. Just take a look at Ghana Jollof, a song by famous Ghanaian musician Sister Derby.

This iconic dish has proven itself as versatile meal to make. The coconut flavor in this recipe will keep you coming back for more and you have arrived at the right place as I will walk you the steps involved to make this dish.

What you will need

  • 1kg beef (or meat of your choice)
  • 5 tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 5 chili peppers
  • 5 red scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 spoon of tomato paste
  • 2 large onions
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 2 whole coconuts
  • 4 bay leaves 'whole'
  • 1 tablespoon dry thyme and 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 5 seasoning cubes
  • 3 cups rice
  • salt
  • vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the stock by rinsing the beef thoroughly and placing it in a pot. Season with crushed seasoning cubes, salt, curry, ground ginger and garlic, as well as chopped onions. Boil till tender and then seperate the meat from the stock and fry in a seperate pan. Keep the stock and oil used for later.
  2. Rinse the rice thoroughly and par boil it for 10 minutes. Rinse the parboiled rice with cold water, drain and set aside.
  3. Now is the time to rinse the tomatoes, chilli pepper, scotch bonnet peppers, and onions. Next, blend everything into a smooth puree. Add the puree to a pot and boil till most of the water is evaporated.
  4. Remove the coconut's flesh and cut into small pieces. Place this into a blender along with some water and blend. Strain off the milk to separate it from the chaff. You can watch this video on how to extract coconut milk.
  5. Add a spoon of tomato paste to the boiled puree. Add the oil used in frying the meat to the puree to fry it, stirring continuosly for 5 minutes to prevent it from burning.
  6. Add the beef stock to the fried puree and mix it well. Add the coconut milk, check the salt and spices level and adjust accordingly.
  7. Gently add the parboiled rice to the sauce, ensuring that the sauce covers the rice. If not, simply add a little water. Add the bay leaves to the pot and cover.
  8. Leave to cook and soften and there you have it!
  9. Serve!
